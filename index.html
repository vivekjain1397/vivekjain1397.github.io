Projects

MindWake - 2014-2015

Technologies used - Arduino, BlueSMiRF Bluetooth software, C, Circuit Maker, 3D Printer

Mindwake is a wearable device which alerts a driver when he/she is losing attention or falling asleep. It utilizes brainwave and other sensor data along with my algorithm to determine if the driver has lost attention or fallen asleep. Data is sent via Bluetooth and interpreted by an Arduino. To test our product, we used a truck simulator and steering wheel. We have a multi-state LED to provide the driver with attention data. If the LED turns red, the driver has completely lost attention and a buzzer goes off to warn him/her. If the LED is yellow, the driver is on the verge of losing attention. If the LED is green, the driver is attentive. Provisional Patent pending.




PinLoco - 2015

Technologies used - Swift (iOS), Parse

PinLoco is a location based iOS app which allows users to take pictures at their current location and post to a map. After that, the user can view the pins of other users in that location and rate the picture they took. The purpose of this app is to allow users to create a sort of trail so other users can view that trail and maybe check out similar locations. The users will also have a private profile where they will be able to save certain photos and follow their friends and view their profiles. This app was created at HackPrinceton 2015 and will be continued.

Experience



UC Berkeley Research Robotic Engineer for 3D Robotics to Map an Unknown Area - 2016

As a freshman at UC Berkeley, I am working with a PH.D student on a robotics research project. At the highest level we are doing mapping with multi-robots. This is one of the two tasks in the Simultaneous Localization and Mapping problem SLAM. The robot teams consist of an observer robot Zumi (camera) and 5-9 picket robots Roaches. The Roaches are fast, cheap but lack computing power and the Zumi is slow, expensive, but has computing power of the smart phone. The Zumi is the leader of each robot team and commands all Roach movements. The roaches role is finding terrain the Zumi can not cross. Based on the orientation and position of the roaches, a map is made of the environment. Roaches will eventually have CO_2 sensors to try to find humans.

I was mainly in charge of the software/hardware aspects of the project. My partner designed the robot and did all of the mechanical engineering tasks. I designed a floor sensor module which would detect what side of the arena and navigate past the obstacles. I also designed the software for the bumper interrupt system which determined what direction the robot should turn after it approached an obstacle.
